Unreal Engine

Unreal Engine is a free* to use software to create almost anything you can image: from virtual worlds to the visually stunning games. There are tones of resources where to start to learn unreal engine but I think the best way, and many will be agreed with me, is the YouTube video from Unreal Sensei. He has not just one but two (or maybe even three) videos 5 HOURS each! And you know what even more insane about it, it is absolutely free. But please do not afraid this amount of time. It is more than just worth it. And this is where you want to kickstart your Unreal Journey.

If you are interested in creating small movies with landscapes, the next step (after you've finished the Sensei's Tutorial) will be this small channel from a very talented and hard-working guy, Amit.

And we cannot talk about Unreal Engine without mentioning this guy. William Faucher. His works are fantastic. So high quality. He has also a lot of stuff on Epic Games Marketplace. Check it out and give this guy a big love he deserves.

Web Dev

Web Development is right now one of the most demandable skill to acquire and one of the cheapest one. By that I mean, you can start to learn and develop your skills without spending none a single cent on lessons (excluding your internet costs and laptop or pc purchase). Because web dev has such a compassionate and kind community, who share their high quality knowledge for free, to make web accessible for all and a better place to be! Please keep that in mind, when you next time write your own website.

Everything starts with and IDE (Integrated Development Environment). A smart word for a simple and at the same time complex "Editor". In our case, Code Editor. As always, there are some awesome editors, and with the time goes by, you'll develop your own preferences but start with Visual Studio Code. You'll love with its endless list of extensions, which makes our code adventures much easier and faster and much much more...

I could throw a hundreds of links, where to start learning web dev but a good point to start for me (and probably it will be for you) was the wonderful book of Shay Howe. He has wrote two of them, one for beginners and one for more advanced topics. I found his work very informative, entertainment and it covers each aspect of web dev. More than just that, he has so many references on pages of hes books, to dig even deeper, if you want. Ah yes, I was very impressed and found it useful. So give it a try to kickstart your web dev career. I've bought his book, even though, he has shared his knowledge for free. Just to be nice.

And also don't forget to look at this sensational documentation from Mozilla and refer to it, if you have any questions.
